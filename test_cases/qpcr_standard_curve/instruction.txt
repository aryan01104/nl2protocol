Prepare a qPCR plate with 8-point standard curve (10-fold dilutions) in triplicate, plus 12 unknown samples in triplicate:

Setup:
- Tube rack A1 contains concentrated DNA standard (1e8 copies/uL)
- Tube rack A2 contains qPCR master mix (keep on temperature module at 4C)
- Tube rack A3 contains nuclease-free water for dilutions
- Sample plate column 1 (A1-H1) and partial column 2 (A2-D2) contain 12 unknown samples

Protocol:
1. Set temperature module to 4°C and wait for temperature to stabilize
2. Prepare standard curve dilutions in column 1 of dilution strip:
   - Add 90uL nuclease-free water to wells A1-H1
   - Add 10uL of concentrated standard to A1, mix well
   - Serial dilute 10-fold: transfer 10uL from A1 to B1, mix, then B1 to C1, etc. through H1
   - This creates standards from 1e7 down to 1 copy/uL
3. Distribute 18uL of master mix (kept cold on temp module) to all wells in the qPCR plate that will be used:
   - Columns 1-3 (rows A-H) for standards in triplicate = 24 wells
   - Columns 4-6 (rows A-H) and columns 7-9 (rows A-D) for unknowns in triplicate = 36 wells
4. Add 2uL of each standard to qPCR plate in triplicate:
   - Standard 1 (A1 of dilution strip) goes to A1, A2, A3 of qPCR plate
   - Standard 2 (B1) goes to B1, B2, B3
   - Continue pattern through standard 8
5. Add 2uL of each unknown sample to qPCR plate in triplicate:
   - Sample 1 goes to A4, A5, A6
   - Sample 2 goes to B4, B5, B6
   - Continue for all 12 samples
6. Mix all reaction wells gently (3 times at 15uL) to combine master mix and template

Use new tips for each standard dilution and each sample to prevent carryover contamination. Master mix can be distributed with the same tip.
