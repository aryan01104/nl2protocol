Set up a cell viability assay testing 6 drug compounds at 8 concentrations each:

Setup:
- Drug plate contains pre-prepared 8-point dose-response dilutions for 6 drugs:
  * Drug 1 in column 1 (A1-H1, highest to lowest concentration)
  * Drug 2 in column 2, Drug 3 in column 3, etc. through Drug 6 in column 6
- Cell plate contains adherent cells at 80% confluency with 100uL media per well
- Reservoir A1 contains fresh complete media
- Reservoir A2 contains MTT reagent (3mg/mL)
- Reservoir A3 contains DMSO solubilization solution

Protocol:
1. Transfer 10uL of each drug dilution from drug plate to cell plate in triplicate:
   - Drug 1 (column 1 of drug plate) goes to columns 1, 2, 3 of cell plate
   - Drug 2 (column 2 of drug plate) goes to columns 4, 5, 6 of cell plate
   - Continue pattern: each drug tested in 3 adjacent columns
   - Use new tip for each drug compound, but same tip within a compound's dilutions
2. For vehicle controls: Add 10uL fresh media to column 11 and 12 (rows A-H) of cell plate
3. Mix all treated wells gently 3 times at 50uL
4. Pause protocol - user removes plate for 24-hour incubation at 37°C, 5% CO2
5. Comment: "After incubation, return plate to deck and resume protocol"
6. Add 20uL MTT reagent to all wells (columns 1-12, rows A-H)
7. Pause for 4 hours MTT incubation (cells reduce MTT to formazan)
8. Carefully aspirate 100uL of media from each well without disturbing the cell layer
9. Add 100uL DMSO to each well to solubilize formazan crystals
10. Mix each well 5 times at 80uL to ensure complete solubilization
11. Pause - user transfers plate to plate reader for absorbance measurement at 570nm
